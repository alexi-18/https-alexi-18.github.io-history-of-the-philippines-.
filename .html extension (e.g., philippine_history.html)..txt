        <section id="spanish">
            <h2>Spanish Colonization (1521-1898)</h2>
            <p>Ferdinand Magellan arrived in the Philippines in 1521, marking the beginning of over 300 years of Spanish rule. This period saw the spread of Christianity, the establishment of towns, and resistance movements like the one led by Lapu-Lapu.</p>
        </section>

        <section id="american">
            <h2>American Period (1898-1946)</h2>
            <p>After the Spanish-American War, the Philippines became a colony of the United States. This era introduced public education, infrastructure improvements, and the eventual push for independence.</p>
        </section>

        <section id="modern">
            <h2>Modern Era (1946 - Present)</h2>
            <p>The Philippines gained independence on July 4, 1946. Since then, the country has experienced various political, social, and economic developments, including martial law, the People Power Revolution, and continuing democratic governance.</p>
